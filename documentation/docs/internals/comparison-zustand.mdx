# Comparison with Zustand

Zustand is the gold standard for minimalist state management in React. While SoulState shares a familiar API and philosophy with Zustand, their internal architectures are fundamentally different. This document explains where they diverge and how to choose the right one for your application.

## 核心 (Core) Difference: Broadcast vs. DAG

### Zustand: Global Broadcast
Zustand's architecture is a **Global Broadcast** system. When any part of the state changes, Zustand iterates through its internal list of all subscribers and checks each one to see if it needs a re-render.
- **Complexity**: $O(N)$ where $N$ is the total number of subscribers.
- **Strength**: High throughput for simple stores and broad updates.
- **Weakness**: Performance degrades linearly as the number of subscribers ($N$) increases, especially for "sparse" updates.

### SoulState: Granular DAG
SoulState is a **Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG)** runtime. Instead of broadcasting, SoulState uses an Invalidation Graph to identify exactly which subscribers are affected by a specific key change.
- **Complexity**: $O(M)$ where $M$ is the number of *affected* nodes.
- **Strength**: **Sparse Update Supremacy**. Propagation time remains constant regardless of total store size, as long as the update impact ($M$) is low.
- **Weakness**: Higher memory and CPU overhead during store creation and subscription registration.

## Feature Comparison

### 1. Dependency Tracking
- **Zustand**: Manual. You must write selectors and ensure they are stable or provide equality functions.
- **SoulState**: **Automatic & Dynamic**. SoulState's tracking proxy detects dependencies at runtime. If your selector logic branches based on state, SoulState updates the graph automatically.

### 2. Batching
- **Zustand**: Relies on React's internal batching or manual `unstable_batchedUpdates`.
- **SoulState**: **Deterministic Microtask Batching**. Batching is a core runtime feature that works outside of React, ensuring consistency across standard listeners, computeds, and components.

### 3. Derived State
- **Zustand**: Typically handled via selectors in components or manual memoization.
- **SoulState**: First-class `computed` API with dependency-aware caching and topological propagation guarantees.

## When to Choose?

### ✅ Choose Zustand if:
- You have a **small-to-medium** number of subscribers (under 5,000).
- You frequently create and destroy stores or subscriptions (high churn).
- You prioritize **minimal bundle size** and simple key-value state.
- Most of your updates affect a large portion of your subscribers.

### ✅ Choose SoulState if:
- You are building an **Enterprise-Scale** application with 10,000+ active listeners.
- Your store is large and updates are typically **sparse** (only affecting a few listeners).
- You have **deeply nested derived state** that requires topological consistency (glitch-free).
- You need high-resolution **instrumentation** to debug propagation bottlenecks.
